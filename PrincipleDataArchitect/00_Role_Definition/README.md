# 00 — Role Definition: The Principal Data Architect

> "A Senior Engineer solves hard problems. A Staff Engineer tells the team which problems are worth solving. A Principal Engineer changes the trajectory of the business."

At the Principal level (L7 at Amazon, E6/E7 at Netflix/FB), you are no longer just a technologist; you are a business leader who wields technology as leverage.

## The Reality of the Role

- **Scope**: Multi-year, organization-wide impact. You own the technical vision for teams of 50-150+ engineers.
- **Autonomy**: You define your own backlog based on where the business is bleeding money or missing opportunities.
- **Ambiguity**: You thrive where requirements are undefined, stakeholders are misaligned, and there is no "right" answer, only trade-offs.

- **Build vs. Buy vs. Partner Frameworks**: The actual TCO (Total Cost of Ownership) math used at Microsoft. Why "we can build it cheaper" is almost always a lie.
- **Managing Technical Debt at the Portfolio Level**: How to quantify tech debt in dollars to get business buy-in for multi-year refactoring.
- **Vendor Lock-in Strategies**: When to embrace it for velocity vs. when to abstract it. (Hint: The "Cloud Agnostic" abstraction layer is usually an anti-pattern).

### `04_Navigating_Organizational_Politics/`

- **Conway's Law in Data Architecture**: Why your data platform looks exactly like your org chart, and how to use organizational design to fix technical problems.
- **The "High-Visibility Failure"**: How Principals navigate and own multi-million dollar outages or failed migrations.
